My self-taught journey with Scrimba.

I’m learning to code through Scrimba—one project, one challenge, and one breakthrough at a time.

When I first started, I had no idea how powerful a few lines of code could be. HTML felt like a foreign language, and CSS? A confusing magic trick. But the more I practiced, the more things started to click. What once felt impossible slowly began to feel intuitive.

What’s made the biggest difference is building real projects—not just watching tutorials. Every component I style, every layout I fix, every bug I debug pushes me forward. Scrimba’s hands-on approach has shown me that you don’t need a CS degree to become a developer—you need consistency, curiosity, and community.

There are days I feel stuck. There are times I doubt myself. But I keep showing up. I keep pushing one line further, one lesson deeper. That’s how progress is made.

I may be self-taught, but I’m not learning alone. I’m building something real—brick by brick—and this journey has only just begun.
